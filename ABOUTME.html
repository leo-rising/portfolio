I've noticed similarities between the structure of Python and Scratch. I feel like learning Scratch's syntax helped set us up for learning Python's (as in, they're both rather strict at times). I feel like Scratch is easier to navigate visually, but that's kind of why it was invented.

I feel like the hardest part about debugging is finding out what exactly went wrong with the program, and how to fix it.
